Step 01 — Problem
A critical trust gap in the core escrow experience
MELLO is a peer-to-peer escrow platform being built to protect buyers and sellers in online marketplace transactions — a space historically plagued by fraud, late delivery disputes, and zero trust between strangers. Early discovery work and user testing reveal a critical gap: once funds are locked in escrow, both parties feel stranded. No feedback loop. No milestone tracking. No way to raise a concern without triggering a full dispute. The experience feels like money disappearing into a black box — and this is a primary driver of hesitation in user testing.
🧪
Step 02 — Research
I interview buyers and sellers to understand the anxiety firsthand
I conduct structured user interviews with buyers and sellers who represent MELLO's target market — people who frequently transact on online marketplaces. The pattern is consistent: the moment funds enter escrow feels like a black box. Users describe anxiety, distrust, and helplessness. Secondary research into competing escrow and payment platforms confirms that transaction transparency — milestone visibility, in-app communication, and real-time status updates — is the single biggest differentiator between platforms users trust and those they abandon. I synthesise findings into a clear problem statement and present them to the product and engineering team to shape prioritisation.
💡
Step 03 — Solution
A Milestone-Based Transaction Tracker
Drawing from my research, I define and champion a Milestone-Based Transaction Tracker — a feature that transforms the flat escrow experience into a transparent, stage-gated workflow. Each transaction becomes a tracked journey, not a black box. Core capabilities currently in active development include:
📊
Transaction Timeline View
A live status bar showing exactly where a transaction sits: Funded → Dispatched → Delivery Confirmed → Released.
✅
Milestone Checkpoints
Sellers mark delivery stages; buyers confirm receipt at each milestone before funds move — reducing all-or-nothing dispute risk.
💬
In-App Messaging Thread
Each transaction has a dedicated communication thread, eliminating external chats that leave no audit trail.
🚩
Dispute Trigger + Evidence Upload
Users can flag a specific milestone and upload proof — shifting disputes from vague claims to documented evidence.
⚙️
Step 04 — Execution
Sprint cycles, tight scope, disciplined delivery
I collaborate directly with the engineering team to scope the feature, write acceptance criteria, and prioritise the MVP version. We work across sprint cycles where I facilitate syncs, manage scope by cutting non-essential sub-features to protect delivery timelines, and ensure the build stays aligned with user expectations established during research. I am also designing the onboarding prompt that will introduce the Tracker to users at their first transaction — a low-friction explainer built to reduce confusion and prevent support tickets before they happen.
🎯

Step 06 — Learnings
The best product work removes anxiety — it doesn't just add features
This work continues to confirm something important: the most impactful product decisions are often invisible to the user. Not a flashy new feature — but the removal of anxiety from an existing flow. My engineering background has been an unexpected asset; the way I think about process stages, feedback loops, and failure modes maps directly onto designing a trustworthy transaction experience. I have also learned to fight for scope discipline — every feature cut in sprint planning is a conscious bet that a clean MVP on time outperforms a bloated v1 shipped late.
